Overview

Chess, which evolved from the ancient game Chaturanga, is revered as the "King of Games." It is a game of deep strategy that has been a part of Indian culture for centuries.

Popularity Today

Today, chess is played in schools, clubs, and online communities across India. With numerous national and international tournaments, the game has seen a surge in popularity among all age groups. Indian chess prodigies like Gukesh Dommaraju have broken records and captured global attention.

Tournaments & Competitions

India hosts various national championships, inter-school tournaments, and even international events. The All India Chess Federation organizes competitions that help nurture young talent, while prestigious events like the Chess Olympiad see India competing on a global stage.

Future & Challenges

As online platforms expand and the game reaches new demographics, chess faces challenges such as ensuring access to quality training and maintaining its classical roots. Nonetheless, initiatives in education and increased media exposure continue to drive its growth.

Why Play Chess?

Chess improves critical thinking, problem-solving, and concentration. With inspiring role models like Viswanathan Anand and Gukesh Dommaraju, the game offers endless learning opportunities and a chance to join a global community of enthusiasts. Whether you play casually or competitively, chess is a game that enriches the mind.
